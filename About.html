About this page

Before I could get started with the project I had to prepare by reading about goal 3 and collect information about the covid vaccine. I also had to prepare the structure and design of the page. Here you can read more about the choices I made during the prosess.

Structure

Files

I began by creating separate folders for the images and the fonts I was going to use during the project. My files are named logically like naming the photo of a kid getting his vaccing, covid_vaccine, which made it easy to find the right files when making the page.

Page structure

I created a navigation menu at the top of the page so that the user easily could navigate between the pages. I decided to make it sticky so that the user always have access to it, even if they scroll to the bottom of the page.

On the bottom of the page I added a footer with the information for NTNU. In the small screens, until 800px, the logo is placed on the top. When the screen is bigger than 800px

Target group

This page is mainly created for kids in school between 6 and 12 years old. I have made my game with the intention to engange school classes in spreading awareness around children health.

The language is easy to understand so that the kids can play around on the site themselves.

Design

Visual hierarchy

The header-tags have different sizes to emphasize the main header and then the different subthemes. This creates a visual hierarchy which makes the content easier to read.

Typeface

The typeface I used on the webpage is Montserrat. I decided to stick with one typeface through my page.

Montserrat is a sans-serif google font. I chose to go for that one because it's easy to read on all screens. Readability is especially important since the page is made for children.

Visuals

I decided to use the same main colors, green and blue, on all my pages. Since the page is ment to educate kids I used soft colors to make it playful, yet "professional".

Green is often assiosiated with environment and is the color i chose with the content related to goal 3. Blue is often assosiated with health, patience and wisdom which is what I wanted kids to think of when reading about the covid virus.

The text contrasts from the backgroundcolor which makes it easy to read for everyone, even if they are visually impaired.

The page width is the same on all elements to add a preception of having a border around the page. This makes the design consistent and structured.

Reflections

When working on the project I ended up devitating somewhat from my original idea. I learned new ways of doing things to acheive the design I wanted. I used my knowledge about graphical design to make design choices for the page, allthough I also tried to keep in mind that I was designing for children. I ended up with a page I find both playful, yet informative.
